Meet the Zombies







Zombies are living humans infected with the Green Flu virus, which causes massive growth
in aggression and the loss of many higher brain functions such as speech and conscience.
The Green Flu Virus can also cause several mutations in which they are call special infected.









Meet the Specials







The Tank is the largest and most powerful of the Infected, The Tank is
extremely tough, slowing only when hit with a massive volume of automatic
weapons fire. The Tank has extraordinary strength and resistance,
flinging concrete chunks ripped from the earth.

When provoked, the Witch is extremely powerful, capable of incapacitating
and even killing Survivors with a single blow. It is agile, capable of
outrunning a survivor if provoked. It is best to stay quiet and avoid close
proximity, as staying close will provoke her.

The Boomer spews a distinct bile at Survivors, briefly blinding them and
attracting a swarm of Common Infected to them. When they die, they burst,
covering any nearby Survivors with bile. At long range, the Boomer isn't
much of a threat; just one solid bullet to it's bloated body and it will die.

The Smoker's fairly long tongue can suffocate Survivors. Smokers, as the
name implies, produce smoke when killed in order to obstruct Survivors'
visibility. The Smoker may use his tongue to drag players towards
themselves, but he remains stationary and therefore cannot move.

The Hunter is very quick and nimble, capable of scaling walls in seconds.
He can also pounce and pin down Survivors, tearing through their abdomens
until the victim dies or the Hunter is pushed off or killed by another
Survivor.

The Spitter shoots a green ball of acid from a great distance, which when
it hits the ground expands into a massive puddle and severely injuring any
Survivors that come into contact with it. The longer the Survivor remains
inside it, the more damage the ooze causes.

The Charger rushes, crashing into the Survivors. If he succeeds, he will take
and carry a single Survivor while pushing and knocking the others down. Once
the Charger hits a wall or other obstacle, or runs a certain distance, he will
start slamming the survivor into the ground to death until an ally frees them.

The Jockey assaults survivors by clinging to them from behind and riding them
while attacking. He can shift his weight around and force the Survivor to fall
in the direction he wants, giving it some control over the Survivor. He will
only let go if his victim is rendered unconscious or if he is knocked off/killed.
